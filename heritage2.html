Amaze of Adam's Peak

About Adam's Peak

Adam’s Peak, also known as Sri Pada, is located in the central highland of Southern Sri Lanka. The name Sri Pada means holy footprint and originates from when Adam set his foot on earth at this place after being cast out of heaven. Thus, the pilgrimage to the temple located at the very top of the mountain. Most travellers start the hike from Dalhousie (Hatton Road) which is the main gateway to Adams Peak. Don’t expect much from the town. The only thing here is some local guesthouses, a few restaurants and small shops where you can buy basic stuff. The town is nothing special and only used as a base for climbing Adams Peak. Adam's Peak is extremely popular with tourists for its alluring beauty and unmatched trekking. The peak, the seventh tallest peak in Sri Lanka, is also known as Sri Pada (sacred footstep) and Samanala Kanda (butterfly mountain).

Best Time To Go

The weather is best from end of December until mid to end of May. This is also when most pilgrims takes the journey, so climbing Adam's Peak during these months means lots of people! Try and stay away from holidays and weekends. January and February are particularly busy. The best time for avoiding too many people is late in the season, April or May, but the weather becomes unstable here and it would be such a shame to stand at the summit covered in clouds. Most people go at night for two reasons. It's cool and thus less exhausting and to see the sunrise from top which is just magical. It's a 2-3 hours hike from Dalhousie and you want to get to the top early to get a good spot (read my tips at the bottom of the article). You should aim at being at the top around 4.30-5.00 am as the sun rises at 6 am. We left Dalhousie around 2 am which was a fair time frame. If you spend the night in Hatton, as some do, leave around midnight by Tuk Tuk. If you are fit you'll be at the top around 3-4 am.

What to expect

The drive in to Dalhousie is incredibly beautiful as you pass through numerous tea plantations and two picture perfect man made reservoirs just before reaching the town at Adam's Peak. Don't expect much of Dalhousie. Other than a few local markets and hostels and hotels there is not much to do and see here. People come for climbing Adam's Peak and nothing else. In the evening time you can begin to look forward to your climb to the top as the path becomes visible under the many hundreds streetlights illuminating the 5.500 steps going all the way to the summit.
